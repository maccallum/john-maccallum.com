...he had probably fallen there during the darkest hours of the night.  The night of a great snowstorm, in which flakes as sharp as blades fell, almost like hail, driven by a furious south wind.  Soaked by that snow, which had first melted and then frozen into shards of ice, the body had been discovered at the foot of a sheer drop, torn by the rocks it had struck on the way down.  <br>

&mdash;Umberto Eco, <i>The Name of the Rose</i>
